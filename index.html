Common questions

Is the AI valuation accurate enough to trust?

Treat it as a defensible range, not a final offer. The AI uses the Doyle log rule, current retail board-foot pricing, and visible cues (species, bark, trunk condition). The final price is set by the mill after on-site inspection. We've found AI estimates land within ±20% of mill offers in most cases.

What trees are actually valuable?

The big winners: black walnut, white oak (especially quartersawn), cherry, sugar maple. Diameter matters — under ~18" DBH, most mills will pass. Hollow trunks, heavy lean, embedded metal (fencing, nails) reduce or eliminate value.

How do I know the mill won't damage my property?

Every mill in our network is verified to carry $1M general liability insurance and provides references. We mediate any disputes. You sign nothing until you accept an offer in writing.

What if my tree isn't valuable?

Most aren't, and we'll tell you that straight up. The AI valuation is still useful — it gives you leverage to negotiate the standard tree-removal quote (cite that the wood will be chipped, ask them to factor the chips' resale into their price).

Where does Plankworth operate?

The AI valuator works everywhere. The mill marketplace starts in OH, IN, PA, MI, IL, NY, and WA — these have the densest portable-sawmill networks. Other states coming as we onboard mills.
